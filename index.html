Limbic System vs Prefrontal Cortex: Internal conflict arises when the amygdala and limbic system push for immediate reward or avoidance, while the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex tries to maintain goal-directed behavior. Naming the conflict reduces amygdala reactivity through a process called "affect labeling."
Habit Stabilizer
Three anchors. Nothing more.

The basal ganglia encode habits through repetition, but overloading it with too many goals creates interference. Three core habits is the neurological optimum for stable encoding without prefrontal overload.

Basal Ganglia Encoding: Habits are encoded in the striatum through repeated execution. Each repetition strengthens the synaptic pathway (long-term potentiation). Limiting to 3 habits prevents "goal competition" where multiple strong intentions fragment attention and reduce successful encoding for each.

Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN): Your master clock in the hypothalamus regulates virtually every physiological process on a ~24h cycle. Light exposure (especially morning light) sets the clock. Irregular sleep timing disrupts cortisol, melatonin, and body temperature rhythms — all of which affect mood and cognition.

Allostatic Load: The cumulative "wear and tear" on your physiological systems from chronic stress. Measured through cortisol levels, HRV (heart rate variability), sleep quality, and inflammatory markers. High allostatic load impairs hippocampal neurogenesis and working memory.
